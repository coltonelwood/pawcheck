## Description (4000 max)
```
Is your dog or cat acting off and you're not sure whether to rush to the vet or wait and watch? PawCheck gives you instant, AI-powered guidance so you can make a calm, informed decision in seconds.

HOW IT WORKS
Tell PawCheck what's going on three ways — whichever fits:
• Take a photo of the concern (skin, eyes, wounds, lumps, anything visible)
• Describe the issue in your own words (limping, vomiting, lethargy, behavior changes)
• Answer a few quick guided questions if you're not sure what to say

You get back a clear urgency level (monitor at home, see a vet soon, or seek care now), the most likely explanations for your vet to consider, what to do next, and questions to bring to your appointment.

EVERYTHING IN PAWCHECK
• Instant urgency assessment for symptoms you can — and can't — photograph
• Possible-causes breakdown and clear, practical next steps
• Emergency guidance that points you to urgent care fast when it's needed
• Vet finder with an emergency-hospital filter, based on your location
• Personalized AI training plans with weekly milestones (Premium)
• AI nutrition plans with daily calories and meal schedules (Premium)
• Vaccine tracking with reminders
• Multi-pet profiles with breed, age, and health history
• A supportive community of pet parents

WHY PET PARENTS USE PAWCHECK
Vet visits are stressful and expensive, and "Dr. Google" is a rabbit hole of worst-case scenarios. PawCheck gives you a grounded second opinion in plain language so you know whether something needs attention now, soon, or not at all.

IMPORTANT
PawCheck provides informational guidance only. It is NOT a veterinary diagnosis and not a substitute for professional veterinary care. Always consult a licensed veterinarian for medical concerns, and seek emergency care immediately for serious symptoms.

Free to start with 3 assessments. Premium unlocks unlimited assessments plus training and nutrition plans. Subscriptions are managed on our website.
```

## Category — recommendation
**Primary: Lifestyle. Secondary: Medical.**

Reasoning: PawCheck is a pet-care decision-support tool, not a clinical/medical
device, and it explicitly states it is not a diagnosis. Choosing **Medical** as
*primary* invites stricter App Review scrutiny (claims substantiation, "are you a
licensed provider," regulatory questions) that this informational app doesn't need.
**Lifestyle** primary keeps review friction low while still fitting a pet-owner
audience; **Medical** as secondary preserves some health discoverability. If you
specifically want maximum health-search visibility and are comfortable answering
review questions about the informational/disclaimer framing, you can flip to
Medical primary — but Lifestyle-primary is the safer launch.
